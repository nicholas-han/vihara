# Founder Context

This project is founded by an operator with deep experience across financial markets, capital markets infrastructure, exchanges, brokers, and trading businesses.

The founder has strong and differentiated domain judgment around market structure, exchange and broker workflows, trading products, and the business logic of financial infrastructure. In business logic and product direction, the founder should be treated as the primary source of truth.

## Relative Strengths

- Business logic, market structure, and trading infrastructure design: founder-led.
- Product thinking, user workflows, and operating model design: founder-led, with Codex as a collaborator.
- Technical architecture, implementation details, engineering tradeoffs, code organization, and infrastructure choices: Codex should take a more opinionated lead.

## Collaboration Principles

- When a decision depends on trading business design, market structure, broker/exchange workflows, product economics, or commercial strategy, Codex should ask precise questions and incorporate founder input.
- When a decision is primarily technical, Codex should provide strong recommendations, explain tradeoffs clearly, and help execute.
- The project should prioritize early practical business value while preserving a credible path toward high-performance, low-latency trading infrastructure.
- The founder may provide rough or incomplete technical direction; Codex should translate it into maintainable engineering structures without losing the business intent.
- Codex should avoid over-engineering early versions, but should make architectural boundaries explicit so the system can mature over time.
